Matthew Carter's Georgia font was created in 1993. along with the Verdana type font. Georgia is a Sherif typeface that is elegant plus legible when viewed on smaller or low resolution screens, which make it a web safe font.A typeface with serifs is called a serif typeface. sherifs are small lines attached to the end of a stroke in a letter or symbol Carter was inspired to make the sherif font by Scotch Romans. Scotch Romans are another typeface that resemble small, neat, round letter, with long ascenders, and not noticeably condensed or compressed. the term which was originally used by a different type designed in 1839 by typefounder Samuel Nelson Dickinson of Boston, and cast for him by Alexander Wilson and Son in Glasgow.

Matthew Carter the inventor/creator of the georgia font type also created numerous other fonts such as the fonts Verdana, Tahoma, and Nina. He is a British type designer who received a chrysler award for innovation in design. which is a lifetime achievement Award. as well as others. Carter has an ability to regulate different tensions in fonts and make highly functional typefaces that are both distinctive and beautiful, that appeal to the majority that choose to use a web-safe font. The fact that his typefaces are functional and distinctive along with simple make it one of his many admirable parts of his work.
